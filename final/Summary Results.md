# Summary of Results

## Project Overview

The Study Zen, Stress & Study Balance Tracker prototype was developed to help students manage academic workload while maintaining their mental well-being. The system combines study tracking, stress monitoring, reminders, and wellness resources into a single platform designed specifically for university students.

The goal of the project was to demonstrate how a simple digital system can encourage healthier study habits and improve awareness of mental health among students.



## Achieved Objectives

The project successfully achieved the main objectives that were defined during the planning stage.



_Monitoring Study Habits_

- Students are able to log and track their study hours, allowing them to monitor their daily and weekly study patterns.

_Tracking Stress Levels_

- The system allows users to record their stress levels and review previous entries. This helps students recognise patterns in their emotional well-being.

_Encouraging Healthy Study Routines_

- Through reminders and dashboard insights, the system encourages students to balance studying with breaks and wellness activities.

_Providing Mental Health Resources_

- The application includes relaxation tips and emergency hotline contacts, giving students access to important mental health support resources.

_Improving Time Management_ 

- Study reminders and session tracking help students organise their study schedules more effectively.

_Providing Smart Recommendations_

- The system provides AI-based suggestions to encourage healthier study behaviour based on recorded study hours and stress levels.


## System Functionality Demonstrated

The prototype successfully demonstrates the following key features:

1. User authentication (login and registration)
2. Dashboard overview of study progress and stress levels
3. Study hours tracking and history
4. Stress level tracking and monitoring
5. Reminder notifications for study and breaks
6. Relaxation and wellness tips
7. Emergency hotline support information
8. Notification system for study and wellness alerts
9. AI-based suggestions based on study hours and stress levels

These features show how the system can support students in maintaining both academic productivity and mental well-being.



## Project Impact

The Study Zen prototype demonstrates the potential of digital tools in promoting healthier study behaviour among university students. By combining productivity tracking with mental health awareness features, the system provides a balanced approach to academic performance and personal wellbeing.

This aligns with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 3: Good Health and Well-Being, which focuses on improving mental health and promoting healthy lifestyles.



## Limitations

As this project focuses on a prototype, several limitations remain:

1. The system is currently developed as a prototype to demonstrate the system features rather than a fully deployed application.
2. AI-based recommendations are simplified and not powered by advanced machine learning.
3. The prototype is limited to demonstration purposes and would require further development for real-world deployment.



## Future Improvements

Several improvements can be made in future versions of the system:

1. Integration with a full database for long-term data storage
2. More advanced AI suggestions based on study and stress patterns
3. Personalised wellness recommendations
4. Integration with university academic systems
5. Mobile push notifications for reminders
6. Data visualisation for deeper analysis of study habits


## Conclusion

Overall, the Study Zen system successfully demonstrates a practical solution for helping students balance academic productivity and mental well-being. By providing tools for tracking study habits, monitoring stress levels, and accessing support resources, the system highlights how technology can contribute to healthier student lifestyles.
